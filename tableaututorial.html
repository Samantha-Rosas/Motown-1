Tableau Tutorial

If you would like to go straight into the visualization portion begin at STEP 11.
1. Download your Data Set onto your desktop
2. Open the file by clicking “Open a Workbook”
PHOTO
3. Once you click “Open a Workbook” it will take you to your Finder (where your files are stored) and select the data set you are going to use.
4. Select "Open"
PHOTO
5. Once the file is open you can filter various aspects of your data set for easier viewing. This is done by clicking the “Add” button under “Filters” in the top right corner of the screen. After clicking “Add” you will be prompted to select what you want to filter.
PHOTO
PHOTO
6. For our data set, we chose to filter by year. Select your choice then click “Ok”.
PHOTO
7. You will then be prompted to select a range for the years.
PHOTO
8. Once you select your range click “Ok”.
PHOTO
9. You may then add another filter or select “Ok” again.
PHOTO
10. Your data is now filtered
PHOTO
If you would like to go straight into the visualization portion start here:
11. Now select the “Sheet 1” tab on the lower left of your screen. Here you can play around with your data with different organization styles.
PHOTO
12. For our data, we are going to create a Text Table so we can see the distribution of music across our filtered dates. We are going to start with the Dimensions Box. Dimensions contain qualitative data, in our case, we will be using ‘Artist Name’ and ‘Track Name’ and ‘Year.’ First, we are going to drag ‘Year’ to the Filter and apply the range of dates from our original filter above.
PHOTO
13. We are then going to add ‘Artist Name’ and ‘Track Name’ to the rows. If this warning pops up, click Add All Members so we don’t lose any data. This step allows us to see all of the tracks clustered together by their respective artist.
PHOTO
14. Next, you will add the ‘Year’ Dimension into the Column section. This allows us to see the distribution of different songs and artists. If we scroll through, we can see any kind of patterns in grouping which allows us to ask questions and research into any thematic similarities between any groups of songs.
PHOTO
15. Now to play around with your data more follow step number 11.
16. For this Sheet we are going to create a treemap. To do this we will select and drag the variables of your choice, in our case this will be “Artist Name” and “Number of Records”, into the rows section.
PHOTO
17. Then select the “Treemap” image on the right side of the screen.
PHOTO
18. Now you can hover over the map and see how many records each artist has.
PHOTO
